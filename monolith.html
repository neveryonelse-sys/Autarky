Thermal Battery / Multifunctional Monolith

Open-Thermal-Monolith • ZeroWaste-Smart-Panels

A single, self-replicating structural material that functions as an energy-positive high-temperature latent-heat thermal battery, high-quality laser source, air/water purifier, and load-bearing building element.

It is designed for zero-waste, zero-gatekeeping replication using only atmospheric CO₂, water, sunlight, common biomass (Chlorella or agricultural waste), mycelium, and regolith/sand (Si source).

Once bootstrapped, the system runs indefinitely without external inputs.

Core Composition

  • Matrix: Mycelium chitin–glucan complex (CGC) aerogel
  • Energy emitter / blackbody radiator: Mg-doped carbon aerogel (FJH-processed from Chlorella hydrochar)
  • Gain medium: N/Si-co-doped carbon dots (CDs) for lasing
  • Thermal storage core: Embedded Si pellets forming molten silicon in SiC shells (~1800 kJ/kg fusion enthalpy)
  • Electrodes: Percolating FJH-graphene network

How It Works

  1. 1. Joule heating via embedded graphene electrodes melts the Si pellets inside in-situ formed SiC containment shells.
  2. 2. Molten silicon releases massive latent + sensible heat, driving the carbon matrix to extreme temperatures and producing intense blackbody radiation.
  3. 3. Thermophotovoltaic cells (TPVs) convert the radiation back into electricity → net positive power output.
  4. 4. Embedded carbon dots create random lasing; diamond Raman converter produces high-quality beam for fabrication and replication.
  5. 5. TiO₂ + carbon dot layers provide photocatalysis for air and water purification.

Zero-Waste Replication Loop

Chlorella/waste biomass → HTC → CDs + hydrochar → FJH → flash graphene/NDs + H₂ byproduct.
Mycelium grown on waste → CGC aerogel → monolith with all components.
Laser beam from the monolith fabricates more units from local materials.

Full Von Neumann-style self-replicating habitats and vehicles

Open-Source Philosophy

Everything is deliberately ungated: no proprietary precursors, no rare elements, no IP. Blueprints, recipes, and replication instructions are meant to be copied freely by anyone with access to sunlight, water, and basic biomass.
