Laser Architecture

Integrated random laser + diamond Raman converter built into the Monolith during a single FJH step

1. Overview

The non-TPV face of the monolith is engineered during the same single arc-welder Flash Joule Heating (FJH) step to function as a high-gain, self-pumped random laser with integrated diamond Raman beam cleanup.

No external optics, mirrors, or gain crystals are required. Everything is created in-situ from the same mycelium/chlorella/hydrochar + Mg/Si precursors. The laser is optically and thermally pumped by the blackbody radiation of the molten-Si/SiC core, producing a high-quality, narrow-linewidth, near-diffraction-limited beam suitable for self-replication and material processing.

2. Asymmetric FJH Engineering (Laser Face Only)

During pre-form preparation perform one additional asymmetric layering step on the laser-facing side only:

  • Mg source: Chlorella bioaccumulated Mg or trace magnesium salt
  • Hydrochar precursor: Longer HTC time (6–12 h) for N/Si-rich hydrochar
  • Layering: Spray or press Mg-doped hydrochar preferentially on the laser face (10–20 wt%)

3. Gain Medium – N/Si-co-doped Carbon Dots + Nanodiamonds

Primary gain: N/Si-co-doped CDs (emission 480–540 nm). Secondary: Flash-nucleated nanodiamonds (NDs) for Raman conversion.

4. Pumping Mechanism

Thermal + optical pumping from the stable high-temperature molten-Si/SiC core. Mg doping broadens emissivity for ideal overlap with CD absorption.

5. Cavity / Feedback Architecture

Random laser cavity created by the 3D porous mycelium-CGC + flash-graphene network (Anderson localization). Distributed feedback from graphene flakes and micro-cavities.

6. Beam Cleanup & Conversion – Diamond Raman Converter

Initial random laser output undergoes stimulated Raman scattering in NDs (1332 cm⁻¹ shift → ~573 nm yellow-orange). Results in dramatically narrowed, spatially cleaned, near-diffraction-limited beam.

7. Performance Characteristics (Projected)

  • Wavelength: Tunable 480–540 nm → 573 nm primary Raman line
  • Power: Watts to tens of watts continuous
  • Beam quality: M² ≈ 1.2–1.5
  • Stability: Latent-heat plateau keeps pump intensity rock-solid

8. Self-Replication Applications

Laser sintering of new monolith preforms, direct writing of electrodes/SiC shells, powering secondary reactors, cutting/welding habitat modules, etc.

9. Why This Architecture Fits the Zero-Waste Philosophy

All components produced from abundant biomass + regolith. No external crystals or rare-earth dopants. The laser emerges naturally from the same FJH step that creates the thermal battery — turning the monolith into a true active replicator.
