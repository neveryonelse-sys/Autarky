Laser Architecture

Integrated random laser + diamond Raman converter built into the Monolith during a single FJH step.

1. Overview

The non-TPV face of the monolith is engineered during the same single arc-welder Flash Joule Heating (FJH) step to function as a high-gain, self-pumped random laser with integrated diamond Raman beam cleanup.

No external optics, mirrors, or gain crystals required. The laser is optically and thermally pumped by the blackbody radiation of the molten-Si/SiC core — turning the monolith into a true multifunctional Von Neumann replicator.

2. Asymmetric FJH Engineering

One additional asymmetric layering step on the laser-facing side only:

  • Mg-doped, longer-HTC hydrochar preferentially on the laser face
  • Bulk core remains standard
  • Opposite face optimized for TPVs

3. Gain Medium

N/Si-co-doped Carbon Dots (tunable 480–540 nm) + flash-nucleated nanodiamonds (NDs) for Raman conversion.

4. Pumping Mechanism

Thermal + optical pumping from the stable high-temperature molten-Si core (blackbody + Mg-broadened radiation).
