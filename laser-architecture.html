Laser Architecture

High-gain, self-pumped random laser with integrated diamond Raman beam cleanup — built directly into the monolith during a single FJH step.

Integrated Random Laser + Diamond Raman Converter

The non-TPV face of the monolith is engineered in the same single Flash Joule Heating step to function as a high-gain, self-pumped random laser with integrated diamond Raman beam cleanup. No external optics, mirrors, or gain crystals are required — everything is created in-situ from the same mycelium/chlorella/hydrochar + Mg/Si precursors already used for the thermal battery core.

Visible-to-NIR Down-Conversion

N/Si-co-doped carbon dots (produced directly from the HTC biomass step) act as the visible-to-NIR converter and gain medium. Longer HTC times (8–12 h) tune the emission to green (\~480–540 nm). These CDs efficiently absorb the visible component of the blackbody radiation and down-convert it to NIR, feeding the final Raman stage far more effectively.

Pumping & Final Beam Quality

The laser is optically and thermally pumped by the intense blackbody radiation and latent-heat stability of the molten-Si/SiC core. The Mg-doped graphene matrix, combined with biomass-derived scattering micro-cubes, produces a high-gain random laser output. This output then pumps a GR1-doped diamond crystal (vacancies created by laser-writing or mild irradiation of bio-CVD diamond). The diamond performs Raman conversion (1332 cm⁻¹ Stokes shift) plus GR1 gain in the NIR (\~700–800 nm), delivering a clean, narrow-linewidth, near-diffraction-limited beam.

Self-Replication & Fabrication Applications

The collimated laser beam is deliberately engineered as the primary fabrication tool of the replicator:

  • Laser sintering of new mycelium/hydrochar/Si preforms into fresh monoliths
  • Ablation and direct writing of electrodes, SiC containment shells, and optical windows
  • Powering secondary FJH reactors or HTC vessels built from previous-generation monoliths

Laser Propulsion Highway

In the long-term space phase, fleets of monoliths can be arranged into a continuous “laser propulsion highway.” Each monolith fires a precisely aimed, high-power beam at the next unit in the chain (or at departing spacecraft), providing continuous laser propulsion without onboard propellant. This creates an efficient, scalable interstellar transport network where the same self-replicating infrastructure that builds habitats also powers high-velocity travel across the solar system and beyond.

The integrated laser turns every monolith into an active fabrication and propulsion node — completing the closed-loop, zero-gatekeeping vision of Autarky.
