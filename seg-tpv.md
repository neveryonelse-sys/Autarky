### TPV Stack with SEG Upgrade – Detailed Layering

When using the SEG upgrade, the TPV stack remains a classic near-field configuration:

**Hot emitter (monolith side)**
1. Carbon aerogel matrix (structural blackbody base)
2. Laser-reacted smooth SiC skin (\~50–200 nm) + embedded asymmetric BCN domains (strong SPhP/HPhP coupling)

**Vacuum gap** (50–100 nm advanced target, 100–500 nm baseline)  
Maintained by EHD-sprayed BN-hydrogel spacers (ImpCarv-style isotropic shrinkage) or laser-machined CGC/SiC pillars.

**Cold receiver panel (SEG-based TPV cell)**
4. Top FJH-graphene layer (\~1–few monolayers) – provides SPP coupling and forms the Schottky junction
5. SEG layer (semiconducting epitaxial graphene, \~0.6 eV bandgap, grown directly on SiC via laser-controlled annealing)
6. Thin SiC substrate (regolith-derived, laser-processed, a few µm to tens of µm thick)
7. Back-surface reflector (BSR) – high-reflectance SiC/graphene multilayer that returns sub-bandgap photons to the emitter
8. Passive radiative cooler (SiC/graphene back layer) + graphene heat-spreading vias

**Why this layering is powerful**  
- The 0.6 eV bandgap of SEG is an excellent spectral match to the \~1414 °C blackbody emitter, dramatically increasing useful photocurrent.  
- SEG’s ultra-high mobility (>5,000 cm² V⁻¹ s⁻¹) improves fill factor and reduces resistive losses.  
- The stiff SiC substrate + graphene top layer provides excellent mechanical rigidity, helping maintain the tiny gap against thermal expansion, vibration, and Casimir forces.  
- Everything is fabricated in-loop from the monolith’s own SiC output and FJH-graphene using the built-in laser for the critical annealing step.

This upgrade path keeps the entire system 100 % abundant (C + Si only) while significantly boosting efficiency and power density.
