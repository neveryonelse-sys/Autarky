# Autarky Atmospheric Water Harvester & Material Regeneration System  
**Permissionless, Zero-Cost-Replicable Self-Sufficiency Module**

**Version:** 2.6 (May 2026)  
**Design Goal:** Build once from scratch, then reproduce and give away the entire working system at essentially zero marginal cost. The recipient inherits a fully closed-loop unit that regenerates itself indefinitely using only local biomass, regolith, and waste heat.

## System Overview

This is a **sorption-based atmospheric water harvesting (AWH)** system that extracts water vapor directly from the air (even at low RH ≥ 10 %).  

The heart of the system is a **Pleurotus-derived carbon-dot (CD) doped, calcium-intercalated graphene oxide (Ca-GOA) aerogel sorbent**. Moisture is captured passively, then released on-demand via a **thermoacoustic chamber** powered by heat from your existing thermal battery. Condensed vapor drips as pure liquid water.

**System Highlights**  
- Modular and portable – stackable trays for easy scaling  
- Bio-circular – oyster shells supply calcium; *Pleurotus* mycelium/fruiting bodies supply CDs and battery interface materials  
- Low-maintenance – reusable for 10–20+ cycles with on-site regeneration  
- Truly universal – works from \~10–90 % RH across any climate  
- Dual-use: AWH + dewatering of wet biomass/waste  
- No sunlight dependency. No external electricity required for core operation.

## Core Sorbent: CD-Doped Ca-GOA Aerogel

**Base Material**  
Graphene oxide (GO) aerogel – highly porous, lightweight (“feather-like”), excellent surface area and interlayer spacing for water intercalation.

**Key Bio-Enhancements**  
1. **Pleurotus-derived Carbon Dots (CDs/CQDs)**  
   - Synthesized via simple hydrothermal treatment of oyster mushroom biomass.  
   - Adds abundant hydrophilic groups (–OH, –COOH, –NH₂ from chitin/β-glucans).  
   - Doping level: 1–5 wt % during hydrogel formation.

2. **Calcium Intercalation (Ca-GOA)**  
   - Sourced from crushed oyster shells.  
   - Creates synergistic hydrogen-bond network (\~3.2× more water per Ca ion than free CaCl₂).  
   - Total uptake: \~2.1 g water per g sorbent.

**Optional Hybrid Boost** (for extreme low-RH <20 % sites):  
Add 5–15 wt % stabilized hygroscopic salt (e.g., LiCl in bio-polymer matrix from oyster/mushroom chitosan) for uptake up to 4+ g/g.

**Resulting Performance**  
- Rapid adsorption even at moderate/low RH  
- High capacity and fast kinetics thanks to CDs + Ca synergy  
- Reusable, lightweight, and naturally antibacterial

## Hierarchical Porosity Enhancement (Optional)

**Purpose**: Further improve sorption–desorption kinetics by creating ordered, low-resistance transport channels — directly inspired by the hierarchical membrane architecture in the May 2026 *Nature Water* paper (EMM-8 zeolite nanosheet membrane).

**How to Implement (Fully Closed-Loop, No New Materials)**  
1. Grow a thin, directionally aligned *Pleurotus* mycelium mat (same directional substrate technique you already use for battery insulation).  
2. Embed or layer the mycelium mat into the GO hydrogel before CO₂ foaming. The chitin network acts as a natural scaffold.  
3. During gelation/foaming and early drying, apply a mild temperature gradient across the tray (warm one side with battery waste heat; cool the opposite side via passive radiative cooling or a simple heat-pipe loop).  
4. Run the thermoacoustic chamber at low power during foaming and drying — the pressure waves help align bubbles and pores along the mycelium scaffold.

**Benefits**  
- Creates anisotropic hierarchical pores (macropores from CO₂ + guided channels from mycelium + nanopores from GO/CDs).  
- Faster vapor diffusion and desorption, potentially pushing daily yields toward the higher end of estimates.  
- Still 100 % regenerable — the mycelium scaffold is grown on-site and refreshed with every new batch.

**Note**: This is optional. The base CO₂-foamed Ca-GOA already provides excellent hierarchical porosity. Add this enhancement once you have validated basic performance.

## Operation Cycle

1. **Adsorption (Passive Capture)**  
   Ambient air flows over the sorbent trays.

2. **Desorption (On-Demand Release)**  
   Heat from your existing thermal battery drives the thermoacoustic chamber. Acoustic waves agitate the aerogel for fast, mild-temperature release.

3. **Condensation & Collection**  
   Released vapor condenses on a cool surface. Pure water collects in a reservoir.  
   **Optional hydrovoltaic layer** for trace electricity.

**Dual-Use Dewatering Mode**  
Place wet biomass on/above the aerogel trays. Thermoacoustic vibration + mild battery heat releases bound water.
