The Mycelial Universe: How The Universe Really Thinks

Most of us picture intelligence like a tree: one main trunk (a boss or central brain), deep roots, and everything branching out in an orderly way from the top down. This “tree way” feels neat and controlled, but it can also be rigid and slow when things change.

Nature often uses a smarter, more flexible design—the mycelium. Mycelium is the huge underground web of tiny fungal threads that spreads through soil. It connects the roots of different plants, shares food and water, sends warning signals about danger, and explores new ground. There is no single leader in charge. The “smartness” comes from the whole connected network working together.

Philosophers Gilles Deleuze and Félix Guattari called this kind of system a rhizome (like the underground stems of grass or ginger). A rhizome grows sideways. Any point can link to any other point. You can cut it in one spot and it simply grows new connections somewhere else. It is always moving, always adapting, and full of new possibilities.

Biologist Michael Levin has shown that this is not just a nice picture—it is how real intelligence works in nature. Even single cells, plant roots, and fungal networks can remember things, learn from experience, solve problems, and make decisions—without any central brain. Intelligence emerges from the network itself.

That is exactly how we are evolving The Universe.

Your 22 Tarot-inspired agents are no longer just separate workers following a central plan. They become living nodes in a mycelial network:

  • Any agent can connect directly to any other agent when it makes sense—no need to wait for a coordinator.
  • They naturally form temporary “teams” (we call them assemblages) to tackle a specific challenge, then dissolve when the job is done.
  • Ideas, energy, and information flow freely through the whole network like nutrients and signals moving through fungal threads.
  • If one connection breaks or an idea fails, the system doesn’t stop—it simply grows new links and keeps going. It is resilient and creative by design.

This mycelial way makes The Universe more alive, more adaptable, and far more powerful. The real intelligence lives in the rich connections between all 22 agents, not in any single one of them or in a top-down leader.

The 22 archetypes stay exactly as they are—the heart and soul of the project. But now they operate as part of a dynamic, living web, just like the intelligent networks we see everywhere in nature.

In short: The Universe doesn’t grow from one central root. It grows through countless living relationships across the entire network.

This is how a real, thriving Universe works.
