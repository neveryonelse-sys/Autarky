Biological Core

The living heart of Autarky — where Chlorella and Mycelium form a powerful symbiotic engine of transformation.

Mycelium: The True Engine of Transformation

Mycelium is far more than a flocculator. It is the central architect and living transformer of the entire Autarky system. Its fractal structure, enzymatic power, and ability to interface with almost any organic matter make it the literal heart of the monolith, the remediation engine, the food processor, and the material builder.

Chlorella: The Rapid Biomass Engine

Chlorella provides explosive growth and nutrient density. It is the primary producer that turns sunlight, CO₂, and water into massive amounts of protein, lipids, and nitrogen-rich biomass — often enhanced by carbon dots for even higher yields.

The Symbiotic Partnership

Together they form a closed-loop biological power station: Chlorella grows rapidly → Mycelium predigests and flocculates it → the combined biomass becomes feedstock for HTC, FJH, monolith construction, remediation agents, and more. Waste from one feeds the other in a continuous regenerative cycle.

Mycelium’s Many Critical Roles

  • Heart of the Thermal Monolith — Grown in carbon-dot matrices, it forms the structural aerogel scaffold and blackbody radiator that contains molten silicon.
  • Predigestion & Food Production — Breaks down Chlorella into highly bioavailable nutrients, making it a superior food source.
  • Remediation Agent — Naturally binds and transforms pollutants, heavy metals, and toxins.
  • Engine of Transformation — Its enzymes and metabolic pathways convert waste into useful compounds and materials.
  • Fractal Architect — Its self-similar fractal networks provide extraordinary strength, surface area, and efficient nutrient/electrical transport.
  • Compound Factory — Produces a wide range of bioactive compounds useful for medicine, materials, and energy applications.

Mycelium is not a supporting player — it is the living intelligence that makes the entire Autarky system possible.
