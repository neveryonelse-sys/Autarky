## Photon-Momentum Engineered Direct-Bandgap Silicon  
**Source**: UC Irvine / *Science*, 31 October 2024  
**Link**: [ScienceDaily summary](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2024/10/241031130403.htm)

**Summary**  
Researchers demonstrated a purely physical method to make ordinary silicon behave as an effective **direct-bandgap** material. By creating sub-3 nm surface asperities and using tightly confined photons to impart extra momentum, they bypass the phonon requirement of indirect-bandgap silicon. Result: up to **10,000× higher light absorption** (and correspondingly higher radiative recombination efficiency) while retaining all the advantages of silicon (abundance, thermal stability, compatibility with existing processes).

**Relevance to the Monolith**  
- **Laser face / PBR illumination**: Could enable higher-efficiency silicon-based LEDs or micro-emitters for finer PAR tuning using the monolith’s own laser for surface texturing.
- **TPV receiver**: Potential future thin-film silicon enhancement or hybrid SEG + direct-bandgap Si layers.
- **Fabrication fit**: The required nanoscale asperities can be created with the monolith’s diamond-Raman laser — fully in-loop, no new materials or tools needed.
- **Timeline**: Most useful in later generations once laser precision and surface control are more mature.

This technique is a strong candidate for future emission-side improvements because it stays 100 % within the abundant C+Si toolkit and leverages the monolith’s existing laser capabilities.
