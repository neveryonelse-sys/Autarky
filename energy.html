Energy Architecture

Graphene and Silicon Thermophotovoltaic (TPV) systems powered by the Multifunctional Thermal Monolith — with integrated supercapacitors for instantaneous power delivery.

Monolith-Powered Energy System

The Multifunctional Thermal Monolith serves as the stable high-temperature heat source and blackbody radiator for the entire energy architecture. Its latent-heat plateau keeps output rock-solid for hours, enabling highly efficient thermophotovoltaic (TPV) conversion on one face while the opposite face produces the integrated laser.

Graphene & Silicon TPVs

Graphene (produced via Flash Joule Heating) and silicon-based TPVs capture the broad-spectrum infrared radiation from the molten-Si/SiC core with exceptional efficiency. The monolith’s engineered emissivity and thermal stability make this one of the simplest and most robust solid-state power generators possible — no moving parts, no turbines, no rare-earth materials.

Supercapacitors Made from the Same Materials & Tools

Using the exact same FJH process and hydrochar feedstock, the system produces high-performance graphene electrodes and aerogel separators for supercapacitors. These supercaps store and deliver massive bursts of power instantly — perfect for laser operation, tool startup, or peak loads — and are fully integrated into the monolith structure. Because they are made from the same abundant biomass-derived carbons, the entire energy storage loop is closed and self-reinforcing.

Closed-Loop Energy Flow

TPV electricity powers the tools (FJH, HTC, PBRs, lasers). Waste heat and off-gases are recaptured. Supercapacitors buffer and smooth power delivery. Excess energy can be used for remediation, material synthesis, or self-replication. The result is a highly efficient, decentralized energy system that requires almost no external input once bootstrapped.

This architecture turns the Monolith into a complete power plant, energy storage bank, and fabrication engine — all from locally available waste and abundant elements.
