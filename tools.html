Tools

The core processing toolkit that turns abundant waste into graphene, advanced materials, energy, and self-replication capability.

The Four Core Tools

Autarky’s toolset is deliberately simple, local, and self-reinforcing. Each tool feeds the others and the Multifunctional Monolith, creating a closed-loop system that requires almost no external inputs after bootstrap.

1. Chlorella Photobioreactors (PBRs)

The rapid biomass engine. Chlorella grows extremely fast and produces high volumes of protein, lipids, and nitrogen-rich biomass. When supplemented with carbon dots produced from its own waste, growth rates can increase dramatically. The PBRs are the primary feedstock generator for the entire system.

2. Hydrothermal Carbonization (HTC)

Converts wet biomass into hydrochar and valuable carbon dots under moderate heat and pressure. The hydrochar serves as feedstock for FJH, while the carbon dots enhance biological growth, photocatalysis, and energy conversion in the monolith.

3. Flash Joule Heating (FJH)

The materials engine. In milliseconds, it turns biomass and hydrochar into high-quality graphene, turbostratic graphene, nanodiamonds, and advanced carbons. These materials are used for electrodes, supercapacitors, thermal battery matrices, coatings, and remediation agents — making the system self-reinforcing.

4. Lasers

Integrated into the monolith itself. The non-TPV face produces a high-gain random laser with diamond Raman beam cleanup. Used for precise fabrication, sintering new monoliths, direct writing of electrodes and SiC shells, and self-replication processes.

Integration with the Multifunctional Monolith

All tools are designed to work in concert with the monolith. The monolith provides stable high-temperature heat and radiation that powers the tools, while the tools supply the materials and energy needed to build and maintain more monoliths. This creates a true self-replicating loop.

These four tools, powered by and feeding back into the Monolith, form the practical foundation that makes Autarky’s vision of decentralized abundance possible.
